# Apply to GitHub Education (Free Student Benefits)

This guide walks you through creating a GitHub account and applying to GitHub Education.
Approved students get the **Copilot Student** plan for free (unlimited code completions plus a monthly allowance for chat and agent requests) and the **Student Developer Pack** (a free domain name for a year, cloud credits, and more).
The clicking takes about 15 minutes, but approval takes days, so do this as soon as you can and well before Day 2.

> 💡 **Tip:** older tutorials say students get "Copilot Pro" for free.
> That changed in March 2026.
> The student benefit is now its own plan called **Copilot Student**.
> Same idea, different name.

One thing to know up front: you do **not** need GitHub Education to publish your website.
GitHub Pages is free for everyone.
If your application is still pending on Day 2, you will use **Copilot Free** instead (see Plan B at the bottom) and nothing about the day is blocked.

## Step 1: Create your GitHub account

Already have a GitHub account? Skip to Step 2.

> ⚠️ **Important:** your username becomes your website address.
> On Day 2 you will publish your site at `https://YOUR-USERNAME.github.io`, and that address is what you will put on college apps and resumes.
> Pick something you will still be proud of in five years.

Some examples:

| Good              | Regrettable           |
|-------------------|-----------------------|
| `jordan-smith`    | `xxdragonslayer420xx` |
| `jsmith-dev`      | `i-hate-homework`     |
| `jordansmith2028` | `sk8rboi-alex`        |

Username rules: letters, numbers, and hyphens only, up to 39 characters, and it cannot start or end with a hyphen or have two hyphens in a row.
Also, this username will be public on the internet, so leave out anything private you would not want strangers to see.

Now create the account:

1. Go to [https://github.com/](https://github.com/) and click **Sign up**.
2. Enter your email address.
   Use an email you actually check, because GitHub will send a verification code there and, later, your Education decision.
   There is also a **Continue with Google** option if you prefer.
3. Create a password and enter your username.
   If the username is taken, GitHub tells you right away.
   Keep trying variations until you find a good one that is free.
4. Follow the remaining prompts, then check your email for a verification code and enter it.
   You should now be signed in and looking at your new GitHub home page.

Email verification is not optional.
Without a verified email, GitHub blocks some basic tasks, including the Education application.

> 💡 **Tip:** GitHub's Terms of Service require you to be at least 13 years old to have an account.
> Everyone in this program (ages 14-18) qualifies.

## Step 2: Check you are eligible

GitHub Education asks for three things.
As a high school student in this program, you almost certainly meet all of them:

- You are at least 13 years old.
- You are enrolled in a degree- or diploma-granting program.
  High schools and homeschool both explicitly count.
- You have a personal GitHub account (you just made one in Step 1).

You will also need **proof of enrollment**.
GitHub accepts any one of these documents, as long as it clearly shows your name and at least one date proving you are enrolled right now:

- A photo of your school ID that shows a current enrollment date.
- A class schedule.
- A transcript.
- An enrollment or affiliation verification letter from your school.

**No school email address? That is normal and fine.**
A school email helps but is not required, and most US high schools do not have an email domain GitHub recognizes anyway.
Plan on using your personal email plus one of the documents above.

## Step 3: Apply to GitHub Education

Before you start, two quirks of the application you should know about:

- **It checks your location.**
  The application asks your browser to share your location and compares it against your school's campus.
  So the most reliable way to apply is while you are physically at school, with location services turned on in both your browser and your computer or phone settings, and any VPN turned off.
- **It uses your camera.**
  Instead of a normal file upload, the application asks you to take a picture of your proof document with your device's camera, right in the browser.
  Laptop webcams often produce blurry, unreadable photos, which is a top rejection cause.
  Doing the whole application on your phone usually works better.

> 💡 **Tip:** best setup = at school, on your phone, location on, VPN off, school ID in hand.

School out for the summer?
That is fine - apply from home with the clearest proof document you have, like a recent transcript or an enrollment letter with a current date.
If you get a location-mismatch rejection, it just means reapplying with a better document.
It does not end your chances.

Now apply:

1. Sign in to GitHub and go to [https://github.com/settings/education/benefits](https://github.com/settings/education/benefits).
   You can also get there by clicking your profile picture, then **Settings**, then **Billing**, then **Education benefits**.
2. Under **GitHub Education**, click **Start an application**.
3. Choose the email address to attach to the application.
   Use your school email if you have one, otherwise your personal email is fine.
4. Type your school's name and pick it from the dropdown.
   If your school is not listed, type its full official name manually and continue.
5. When asked, allow the browser to share your location.
6. When prompted for proof, hold your document up to the camera and take the picture.
   Check the preview.
   If you cannot read the text in the photo, neither can the reviewer.
   Retake it until your name and the date are sharp.
7. Click **Submit application**.
   You should now see a confirmation that your application is under review.

## Step 4: While you wait

- **How long?**
  Usually about 2-3 business days for a decision, then the benefits themselves can take a few more days to switch on.
  At busy times (like the start of a semester) it can stretch to about two weeks.
  These are typical numbers, not a guarantee.
- **How will I find out?**
  The decision arrives by email at the address you put on the application.
  You can also check the status anytime at [https://github.com/settings/education/benefits](https://github.com/settings/education/benefits).

**If you get rejected, do not panic.**
Rejections are common and you can reapply right away - GitHub documents no waiting period.
The usual causes and fixes:

| Rejection reason                                                   | Fix                                                                                                                                                                                                           |
|--------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Document unclear, or no readable date on it                        | Retake the photo with your phone. Make sure your name and at least one current date are sharp and in frame. Try a different document (school ID with date, class schedule, transcript, or enrollment letter). |
| Location mismatch                                                  | Reapply while physically at school, with browser and device location services on and any VPN off.                                                                                                             |
| School email domain not recognized                                 | Reapply with your personal email plus a proof document instead.                                                                                                                                               |
| Email already used for a Student Developer Pack on another account | One academic email can back only one pack account, ever. Use a different email that has never been tied to the pack.                                                                                          |

To reapply, go back to [https://github.com/settings/education/benefits](https://github.com/settings/education/benefits), click **Start an application** under **GitHub Education**, and submit again with the fix applied.

## Step 5: After approval - turn on Copilot Student

Approval does **not** automatically turn on Copilot.
You claim it as a separate step:

1. Go to [https://github.com/settings/education/benefits](https://github.com/settings/education/benefits).
2. Under **Free GitHub developer resources for students and teachers**, select **Learn more**.
3. Follow the prompts to activate **Copilot Student**.
4. If you already set up VS Code, restart it and sign back in to Copilot so the editor picks up your new plan.
   Setup steps are in [02_setup_vscode_and_copilot.md](02_setup_vscode_and_copilot.md).

What you get with Copilot Student: unlimited code completions, 200 monthly AI Credits for chat and agent requests, with the model chosen automatically for you (there is no manual model picker on this plan).
GitHub rechecks your student eligibility every month, so keep your account in good standing.

> ⚠️ **Important:** GitHub paused new Copilot Student activations in spring 2026 and has been reopening them gradually.
> If the claim flow says sign-ups are paused, you did nothing wrong.
> Use Copilot Free for now (Plan B below) and retry the claim every few days.

If you claimed Copilot Student but VS Code still shows the Free plan after a few hours, sign out and back in.
If it still has not appeared after about 5 days, contact GitHub support at [https://support.github.com/contact/education](https://support.github.com/contact/education) with the topic "Copilot Student".

While you are at it, browse the rest of the [Student Developer Pack](https://education.github.com/pack).
The most useful offers for your website: Namecheap gives you one free domain name for a year, and DigitalOcean gives you $200 in cloud credit for a year.
(Skip the Azure credit offer.
It is for students 18 and older.)

## Plan B: Copilot Free (no application needed)

If your Education application is pending, rejected, or the Student plan is paused, you still have Copilot.
**Copilot Free** works on any GitHub account, needs no application, and needs no credit card.

What it includes per month:

- 2,000 code completions (the gray ghost-text suggestions while you type).
- 50 chat requests, and agent-mode prompts count against this same 50.
- Models are picked automatically. There is no manual model picker on Free.

How to enable it: nothing special.
When you sign in to Copilot inside VS Code with a GitHub account that has no Copilot plan, it enrolls you in Copilot Free automatically.
The exact clicks are in [02_setup_vscode_and_copilot.md](02_setup_vscode_and_copilot.md).
If any flow asks you for payment info, back out.
You are in the wrong flow (probably a paid trial), because Copilot Free never asks for a card.

Honestly: 50 chat and agent requests is tight for a full afternoon of vibe coding, and the allowance resets monthly, not daily.
But the Day 2 project is absolutely doable on Free if you spend requests wisely.
Start from the starter template in [`../starter/`](../starter/) so you do not burn requests scaffolding a page from scratch, and follow the prompting tips in [04_vibe_code_your_website.md](04_vibe_code_your_website.md), which are written with the Free plan in mind.
Completions and chat are separate pools, so even if you run out of chat requests, your 2,000 monthly completions keep working (and vice versa).

## Troubleshooting & FAQ

**My application has been pending for over a week.**
That happens at busy times. Check the status at [https://github.com/settings/education/benefits](https://github.com/settings/education/benefits) and keep an eye on the email you applied with.
Meanwhile, Day 2 works fine on Copilot Free.

**I do not have a school email address.**
Totally fine. Apply with your personal email and a proof document (school ID with a date, class schedule, transcript, or enrollment letter).

**I am under 13.**
GitHub's Terms of Service do not allow accounts for anyone under 13, and GitHub terminates accounts it finds.
There is no workaround.
Talk to an instructor.
(Everyone in this program is 14-18, so this should not apply to you.)

**I was rejected and the email mentioned my document.**
The reviewer could not read a current date on it. Retake the photo with a phone camera and make sure your name and at least one current enrollment date are clearly visible, or switch to a different document from the list in Step 2.

**I applied from home and got rejected.**
Probably the location check. Reapply from school with location services on and any VPN off.

**Does a pending application block me from publishing my website?**
No. GitHub Pages is free on every account.
You can do all of [03_publish_your_first_page.md](03_publish_your_first_page.md) with zero Education benefits.

**I got approved. Where is my Copilot?**
You have to claim it separately. Go back to Step 5.
Benefits can also take up to about 72 hours after approval to switch on.
